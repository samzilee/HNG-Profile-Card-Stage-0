About Me

Bio

I’m a self-taught web developer who started learning during my SIWES program when my original placement fell through. I’ve built several hands-on projects including a movie website, a contact saver app in React, and a game that challenges players to guess a bar’s width. I enjoy learning by doing experiment with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Tailwind CSS, and watching how small ideas grow into full, interactive applications.

Goals

My main goal in this program is to strengthen my frontend development skills, especially around accessibility, responsiveness, and clean UI design. I also want to get comfortable building complete applications from a simple profile card to multi-page apps and gradually prepare myself for backend development using Node.js and databases like MySQL or Appwrite. Ultimately, I aim to become a full-stack developer who can build practical, real-world projects confidently.

Areas of Low Confidence

I still feel a bit unsure when it comes to advanced form validation, backend integration, and optimizing app performance. Sometimes I need more practice with accessibility (ARIA labels, keyboard navigation) and writing cleaner, more maintainable code. But I’m improving steadily through each challenge, mistakes are just part of my learning process.

Note to future Self

Keep pushing your limits and don't be afraid to ask for help when you need it. Remember that every expert was once a beginner, and the journey is just as important as the destination.

Extra Thoughts

This journey has taught me that persistence is more powerful than talent. Every time I struggled with a concept, I found an answer through patience and practice. Frontend Wizards challenges like this push me to think like a real developer focusing not just on looks, but on structure, accessibility, and user experience. I’m proud of how much I’ve learned, and I’m excited for what’s next.
